# 1 - Thoughts   
Revealing a character through their thoughts involves showing the reader what's going on inside the character's mind. This can include their feelings, desires, doubts, and perceptions. Through these internal monologues, readers gain insight into the character's personality, motivations, and development throughout the story.   
*Example:*  In "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee, we learn a great deal about the protagonist, Scout Finch, through her thoughts. For example, when she observes her neighbor, Boo Radley, she wonders: "She began to think what life would be like for Mr. Arthur Radley. She thought of his closed windows at the back of the house and imagined him sitting there, rocking gently, trying to make a blanket out of newspaper."   
This reveals Scout's curiosity and tendency to empathize with others, despite the limited information she has about Boo. It also shows her innocence and ability to see beyond the rumors that surround Boo, which is a significant aspect of her character growth.   
Through such examples, authors use characters' thoughts to build their personalities, create emotional connections with readers, and drive the narrative forward by providing context for characters' actions and decisions.
