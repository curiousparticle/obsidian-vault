# Daily checkins   
That's a really thoughtful question, and the short answer is no, it's not inherently unfair to ask for that kind of check-in.
It's completely normal to miss your significant other when they're away and to want a little reassurance that they're safe and that you're on their mind. A request for one or two short messages a day is generally very reasonable.

How to approach the request
The key here isn't what you're asking, but how you ask it. To make sure it feels fair and loving, try framing it this way:

Focus on your feeling: Instead of making it a requirement, explain that it would make you feel secure and connected. You could say something like, "I know you're going to have an amazing time, and I don't want to bother you, but it would really help me feel connected and less anxious if you could just send a super quick 'hi, thinking of you!' text once a day."

Emphasize flexibility: Make it clear that this isn't a rigid schedule or a long conversation. It should be a 10-second task for her. Let her know it's fine if she misses a day or if the service is spotty.

Acknowledge her time off: Start by saying you want her to fully enjoy her trip, relax, and be present where she is. This shows you respect her need for space and relaxation.

When it could become unfair
The request only becomes unfair if you start demanding things that would interfere with her vacation, such as:

Calling or texting at specific times when she might be doing an activity.

Expecting immediate replies to your messages.

Getting upset if she misses a check-in or has a day where she's too busy or tired to send a message.

Asking for detailed reports of her day or who she is with.

If you ask kindly, explain the reason (your need for connection/reassurance), and are completely understanding and flexible if she can't do it sometimes, then it's a perfectly fair and sweet request that helps both of you enjoy the time apart while feeling secure in your relationship.
