# Foods to Avoid During a Cold    
To help prevent that "frog in the throat" sensation tonight, you want to avoid anything that either increases mucus production or makes your current mucus thicker and "tackier."
Here is what you should steer clear of for the rest of today and this evening:   
1. Dairy Products (The "Thickener")
While dairy doesn't necessarily make you produce more mucus, it contains a protein called beta-casomorphin-7 that can make existing mucus feel much thicker and more difficult to swallow.   
    - Avoid: Milk, cream-based soups, cheese, and ice cream.   
    - Why: This will prevent that "sticky" feeling that makes you want to clear your throat or hum to get rid of the itch.   
2. Refined Sugars
Sugar can trigger inflammation and, for some people, can actually increase mucus production.   
    - Avoid: Soda, heavy desserts, or sugary candies.   
    - Why: High sugar intake can dry out the mouth and leave a film in the throat that mimics the feeling of post-nasal drip.   
3. Caffeine and Alcohol
Both are diuretics, meaning they dehydrate you.   
    - Avoid: Coffee, black tea, and alcohol after 4:00 PM.   
    - Why: Dehydration is the enemy of a dry cough. When you are dehydrated, the lining of your throat becomes more "twitchy" and sensitive to the air you breathe, which triggers the cough reflex. Alcohol also relaxes the throat muscles, which can actually make post-nasal drip settle deeper into your airway.   
4. Very Salty Foods   
    - Avoid: Heavy snacks like potato chips or very salty processed meats.   
    - Why: Excess salt can dry out your throat tissues quickly, leading to that sharp "tickle" that kept you up last night.   
5. Spicy or Acidic Foods (The "Silent" Trigger)
Sometimes, what feels like a "cough" is actually Silent Reflux (LPR). When you lie down, stomach acid can travel up and irritate the throat, causing an itch.   
    - Avoid: Citrus fruits, tomatoes, spicy peppers, and chocolate.   
    - Why: These can irritate the esophagus and make your throat feel even more "swollen" or itchy.   
   
## What SHOULD you eat/drink tonight?   
- Clear Broths: Chicken or vegetable broth is excellent for thinning mucus and hydrating the throat.   
- Warm Water with Lemon and Honey: The lemon helps break up mucus, and the honey coats the throat to protect those sensitive "itch" receptors.   
- Soft Fruits: Things like melon or berries are hydrating and easy on the throat.
Pro-Tip: Try to have your last meal at least 2–3 hours before you plan to lie down. This ensures your stomach is empty, reducing the chance of any reflux-related coughing.
