# Medicine Nighttime Cold Regimine   
I am so glad you finally caught some sleep! Five hours isn't a full night, but it’s enough to break that "exhaustion cycle" and help your body start recovering.
Since the "frog in the throat" and post-nasal drip kept you up until dawn last night, the goal for today is to prevent the buildup before you even hit the pillow.   
1. The "Early Evening" Drying Phase
Don't wait until 10:00 pm to treat the drip.   
    - Switch to a Non-Drowsy Antihistamine: If you have one (like Claritin, Allegra, or Zyrtec), take it around 4:00 or 5:00 pm. This helps dry up the mucus production before it has a chance to pool in your throat when you lie down.   
    - Hydrate Now, Taper Later: Drink plenty of water throughout the afternoon to keep mucus thin. Stop drinking large amounts about 2 hours before bed so you aren't waking up to use the bathroom.   
2. Clear the Pipes Before Bed   
    - The "Neti Pot" or Saline Rinse: If you have a saline nasal spray or a sinus rinse, use it about an hour before bed. This physically washes away the allergens and mucus that cause the "itch" on the side of your throat.   
    - The Vertical Steam: Take your warm shower again, but this time, follow it with a warm salt water gargle immediately. This cleans out any "tacky" mucus the steam loosened up.   
3. Setup Your "Sleep Station"   
    - Check Your Pillows: Make sure your "elevation" is actually supporting your upper back, not just cranking your neck forward. If your chin is tucked too hard toward your chest, it can actually trigger the cough reflex.   
    - Temperature: Keep the room slightly cooler (around 18 c). Very warm air can be drying, which triggers that "non-productive" hack.   
4. Medication Timing (The "Swiss" Strategy)
Since you have Triofan and Benadryl:   
    - 30 Minutes Before Bed: Take the Benadryl. This will address the drip and the "itch" while helping you fall asleep.   
    - At Bedtime: Use the Triofan (Butamirate). It acts on the cough center in the brain to quiet the reflex.   
    - The Nasal Spray: Use it right before you turn out the lights to ensure your nose stays clear for the first few hours of sleep.   
5. The "Emergency" Nightstand Prep
To avoid having to get out of bed at 3:00 am:   
    - A thermos of warm (not hot) water: Cold water can sometimes shock the throat into a cough; room temp or warm is gentler.   
    - Hard candy or cough drops: Sucking on something increases saliva, which is the best natural way to "clear the frog."
