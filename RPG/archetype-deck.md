# Archetype Deck   
Absolutely! Below is a **customized archetype card deck** for *Deserts of Desolation*, designed to help players quickly create engaging, campaign-relevant characters. The deck is divided into **five categories**, each with 4-6 options. Players can draw or choose **one card from each category** to build a character with depth, ties to the setting, and immediate hooks for the adventure.   
 --- 
### Deserts of Desolation Archetype Card Deck   
*(Print or use digitally. Players draw/choose 1 card per category.)*   
 --- 
### 1. Role in the Story   
*(Who are you in this epic quest?)*   
- **The Cursed Scholar**: A historian or mage obsessed with uncovering the secrets of Amun-Re’s curse—or breaking it. *"I’ve read every scroll about the Star Gems... but some knowledge is better left buried."*   
- **The Exiled Guardian**: A former temple guard or Dervish, disgraced or fleeing a blood feud. *"I swore to protect the tombs. Now I’m the only one who can rob them."*   
- **The Mercenary Survivor**: A soldier or caravan guard who barely escaped the Efreet’s wrath. *"I don’t care about prophecies. I just want to live—and maybe get rich doing it."*   
- **The Reluctant Heir**: A noble or merchant’s child, forced into the desert to prove their worth. *"My family sent me here to ‘learn humility.’ They’ll regret that."*   
- **The Oathbound**: Sworn to a dying comrade, a god, or a lost love to complete the quest. *"I promised I’d see this through. Even if it kills me."*   
- **The Thief of Fate**: A rogue who stole an artifact tied to the curse—and now the desert itself seems to hunt them. *"That amulet was just a trinket... until the sand started whispering my name."*   
 --- 
   
### 2. Flaw/Virtue   
*(What defines your choices?)*   
- **Greedy but Loyal**: *"I’d sell my soul for gold... but I’d spend it all to save a friend."*   
- **Brave but Reckless**: *"I charge into every fight. Sometimes I win. Sometimes I wake up in a pit of scorpions."*   
- **Cunning but Distrustful**: *"I outsmarted a djinn once. Now I trust no one—not even my shadow."*   
- **Compassionate but Naïve**: *"I see the good in everyone. Even the mummy trying to eat my face."*   
- **Stoic but Haunted**: *"I don’t flinch at blades or curses. But the desert’s silence... that’s what breaks me."*   
- **Charismatic but Secretive**: *"I can talk my way into a king’s vault. Just don’t ask what I’m hiding in my sleeves."*   
 --- 
   
### 3. Signature Item   
*(What do you carry that defines you?)*   
- **A Copper Medallion**: Stamped with the palm-tree symbol of the Oasis. *"A dying Airlancer pressed this into my hand. I don’t know why."*   
- **A Vial of Black Sand**: From the Cursed City of Stone. *"It whispers when the Efreet is near."*   
- **A Broken Compass**: Points to the last Star Gem you touched. *"It spun wild the day the sky burned."*   
- **A Dagger of Glass**: Forged from Skysea’s ruins. *"It cuts spirits as easily as flesh."*   
- **A Locket with a Portrait**: Of someone you failed to protect. *"Their last words were ‘Find the Sphere.’"*   
- **A Scroll of Forbidden Prayers**: Written in the ancient alphabet. *"The Dervishes would kill me for reading it. I’ve read it twice."*   
 --- 
   
### 4. Secret   
*(What are you hiding?)*   
- **Wanted for a Crime You Didn’t Commit**: *"The sheik’s men think I stole the bride. I wish I had."*   
- **Haunted by a Ghost**: *"A child from the Sunken City follows me. She won’t say why."*   
- **Heir to a Ruined House**: *"My family ruled Pazar. Now I’m all that’s left—and the Efreet knows it."*   
- **Cursed by the Star Gems**: *"I touched one. Now my dreams are full of fire and screaming."*   
- **A Deal with a Djinn**: *"I traded my voice for a way into the tomb. I’d do it again."*   
- **The Last Keeper of a Dying Faith**: *"I’m the only one who remembers the old gods’ names. And they’re calling me."*   
 --- 
   
### 5. Relationship to the Party   
*(How are you connected?)*   
- **Owes a Debt to Another PC**: *"You saved my life. I’ll return the favor—even if it gets us both killed."*   
- **Hates One PC’s Faction**: *"Your Sandvoyager friends left me to die in the dunes. I haven’t forgotten."*   
- **Sworn to Protect a PC**: *"The oracle said you’d die without me. I’m starting to think she lied."*   
- **Shares a Vision with a PC**: *"We both dreamed of the Efreet’s face the night the stars fell."*   
- **Rivals for the Same Goal**: *"We both want the Star Gem. Only one of us is walking out of here with it."*   
- **Bound by a Curse**: *"We touched the same relic. Now our fates are linked—literally."*   
 --- 
   
### How to Use the Deck:   
1. **Draw or Choose 1 Card per Category**: Players can randomize (draw blindly) or pick intentionally.   
2. **Spend 5 Minutes Answering**:   
    - *"How did you get your signature item?"*   
    - *"Who knows your secret?"*   
    - *"What’s one thing your flaw has cost you?"*   
3. **Share Your One-Sentence Pitch**:   
    > "I’m a cursed scholar who’s too trusting for my own good, carrying a vial of black sand that whispers to me. I owe my life to Joruna, but I’m hiding that the sand is slowly turning me to stone."   

4. **GM Ties It to the Campaign**:   
    - The **vial of black sand** reacts to the Efreet’s presence (foreshadowing).   
    - The **copper medallion** is a clue to the Oasis of the White Palm.   
    - The **rivalry over the Star Gem** creates tension during the pyramid heist.   
 --- 
   
### Example Character (Drawn from the Deck):   
- **Role**: The Thief of Fate   
- **Flaw/Virtue**: Charismatic but Secretive   
- **Signature Item**: A Scroll of Forbidden Prayers   
- **Secret**: A Deal with a Djinn   
- **Relationship**: Sworn to Protect a PC   
   
**Backstory**:   
> "I stole the scroll from a Dervish temple—then sold it to a djinn for a way into the tomb. The djinn laughed and said, ‘The tomb will take more than your voice.’ Now I’m bound to protect Kipper, the halfling who picked the lock that night. The scroll’s prayers are the only thing that keeps the djinn’s bargains from twisting... but I haven’t told Kipper that yet."   

**GM Hooks**:   
- The **djinn** reappears when the party faces the Efreet, offering "help" (with strings attached).   
- The **scroll** can be used to **disrupt Munafik’s magic** in the pyramid—but each use ages the reader 1d10 years.   
- Kipper’s **kleptomania** might lead them to steal the scroll, triggering the djinn’s wrath.   
 --- 
   
### Why This Works for Deserts of Desolation:   
- **Ties to the Plot**: Every card references the **Star Gems, Efreet, Oasis, or tombs**.   
- **Fast and Fun**: Players can build a character in **10 minutes** with immediate depth.   
- **Collaborative**: Relationships and secrets create **party dynamics** organically.   
- **GM-Friendly**: Each card is a **plot hook** (e.g., the vial of sand → Efreet sensor; the medallion → Oasis clue).
