# Herbal Healing   
Here’s a **detailed, playable system** for herb-based healing in your DCC (or OSR) game, designed to decentralize healing, encourage resource management, and keep the game dangerous but fair. This system replaces cleric-exclusive healing with craftable poultices and drafts, while still giving clerics (or dedicated healers) an edge.   
 --- 
### Herbal Healing System   
**Core Principles:**   
- Healing is slow, requires resources, and is accessible to all—but skilled healers (like clerics) are more effective.   
- Poultices and drafts are consumable, limited, and must be crafted or found.   
- Healing happens over time (e.g., per hour), not instantly, to maintain tension.   
 --- 
   
### 1. Healing Poultices & Drafts   
### Basic Poultice (Anyone Can Craft)   
- **Effect:** Restores **1 HP per hour** for **1d4 hours** (total: 1-4 HP).   
- **Ingredients:** 1 unit of common herbs (e.g., woundwort, yarrow, or comfrey).   
- **Crafting:** Requires 10 minutes of work and a successful **DC 10 Intelligence or Luck check**. Failure wastes the herbs.   
- **Portability:** Poultices are bulky; a character can carry up to 3 at a time.   
   
### Healer’s Poultice (Clerics/Healers Only)   
- **Effect:** Restores **1d3 HP per hour** for **1d4 hours** (total: 1-12 HP).   
- **Ingredients:** 1 unit of common herbs + 1 unit of rare herbs (e.g., goldenroot, moonbloom) **or** holy water.   
- **Crafting:** Requires 10 minutes and a successful **DC 12 Intelligence or Luck check**. Clerics add their level as a bonus to the check.   
- **Portability:** Same as basic poultices (max 3).   
   
### Healing Draft (Anyone Can Craft, but Risky)   
- **Effect:** Restores **1d4 HP immediately**, but the drinker must pass a **DC 12 Fortitude save** or suffer **1d4 HP damage** from toxicity (or nausea for 1 hour).   
- **Ingredients:** 1 unit of common herbs + 1 unit of alcohol (or purified water).   
- **Crafting:** Requires 1 hour and a **DC 12 Intelligence check**. Failure creates a poisonous brew (1d6 damage if consumed).   
- **Portability:** Drafts are liquid; a character can carry up to 2 vials.   
 --- 
   
### 2. Tracking Healing Over Time   
- **Hourly Recovery:** Poultices provide healing **per hour of rest**. Players must track:   
    - **Active Poultices:** Note which characters have poultices applied and how many hours remain.   
    - **HP Recovery:** At the start of each hour (or game turn, if tracking time closely), restore the listed HP.   
- **Example:** A fighter applies a **basic poultice** (1 HP/hour for 3 hours). Over 3 hours of rest, they recover 3 HP total.   
- **Downtime:** If the party is in a safe location (e.g., town, camp), they can apply poultices and rest for multiple hours to recover more HP.   
 --- 
   
### 3. Finding & Managing Herbs   
- **Foraging:** Characters can search for herbs in the wild. A successful **DC 12 Intelligence or Survival check** yields 1d4 units of common herbs. Rare herbs require a **DC 15 check** and are only found in specific locations (e.g., sacred groves, caves).   
- **Purchasing:** In towns, common herbs cost **1 gp/unit**; rare herbs cost **10 gp/unit**.   
- **Spoilage:** Herbs spoil after **1d4 weeks** unless preserved (e.g., dried, stored in airtight containers).   
 --- 
   
### 4. Clerics & Healers: Still Special   
- **Divine Blessing:** Clerics can **bless a poultice** as a ritual (10 minutes, no check required), doubling its duration (e.g., 1d4 hours → 2d4 hours).   
- **Lay on Hands:** Once per day, a cleric can stabilize a dying character (stop bleeding) with a touch, no herbs required.   
- **Prayer for Recovery:** Clerics can pray for a dying character to recover 1 HP (once per character per day), but this requires a **DC 14 spell check**. Failure means the character stabilizes but gains a temporary flaw (e.g., "Haunted by Visions" for 1 day).   
 --- 
   
### 5. Optional Rules for Grit & Realism   
- **Injury Penalties:** Characters with poultices applied might suffer minor penalties (e.g., –1 to Dexterity checks due to stiffness) until fully healed.   
- **Addiction Risk:** Overusing drafts (more than 3 in a week) requires a **DC 12 Fortitude save** or the character becomes dependent (–1 to all rolls until they abstain for a week).   
- **Scars & Trauma:** Even with poultices, dropping to 0 HP always leaves a mark. Roll on an injury table for permanent or long-term effects.

### 7. Suggestions for Implementation   
1. **Start Simple:** Introduce basic poultices first. Add drafts and healer’s poultices as players get comfortable with the system.   
2. **Herb Economy:** Make herbs a **limited resource**. Players will need to plan, forage, and prioritize who gets healed.   
3. **Encourage Roleplay:** Describe the process of applying poultices (e.g., "The woundwort’s bitter scent fills the air as you bind the poultice to your arm").   
4. **Cleric Flexibility:** Let clerics choose between being **combat-focused** (fewer healing abilities) or **healer-focused** (better poultices, but weaker in combat).   
5. **Worldbuilding:** Tie herbs to your setting. For example:   
    - Woundwort grows near battlefields.   
    - Goldenroot is guarded by druids.   
    - Holy water must be blessed at a temple.   
 --- 
   
### 8. Example Play Scenario   
> After a brutal fight, the party’s thief is at 2 HP, and the warrior is at 0 HP (dying). The cleric has 1 unit of goldenroot and 2 units of woundwort. The thief forages and finds 1 more unit of woundwort (DC 12 check).   

- The cleric crafts a **healer’s poultice** (1d3 HP/hour for 1d4 hours) for the warrior, stabilizing them and restoring 2 HP over 3 hours.   
- The thief applies a **basic poultice** to themselves, recovering 1 HP per hour for 2 hours.   
- The party rests for 3 hours, recovering a total of 3 HP (thief) and 6 HP (warrior, thanks to the healer’s poultice).   
 --- 
   
### Why This Works   
- **Teamwork:** Everyone can contribute to healing, but clerics/healers are still valuable.   
- **Resource Management:** Players must balance herb use, risk, and downtime.   
- **Danger Remains:** Healing is slow, so battles and traps stay deadly.   
- **Immersion:** Crafting and foraging add depth to the world and encourage exploration.   
 --- 
   
**Final Thought:**
This system shifts healing from "cleric casts a spell" to **"the party works together to survive."** It’s easy to track, keeps the game tense, and makes every HP count.
