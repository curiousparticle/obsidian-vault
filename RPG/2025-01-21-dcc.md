# 2025-01-21 DCC   
This is a **fantastic** twist—it adds urgency, tactical depth, and a clear path for the players to reunite and take action. Here’s how you can structure the scene for maximum drama and player engagement:   
 --- 
### 1. The Obelisk Lands: Immediate Chaos   
- **Landing Impact:** The obelisk crashes onto the glacier, shaking the ice and causing minor avalanches. The ground trembles, and the players inside Null-Eleven’s hideaway hear the commotion above.   
- **Riders Deploy:** Beakmen and croc knights pour out of the obelisk, fanning out in all directions—including a small scouting party heading toward the hideaway’s entrance. The rest ride off to explore the area, leaving only a skeleton crew to guard the obelisk.   
 --- 
   
### 2. The T-Rex Distraction   
- **Null-Eleven’s Last Defense:** The hideaway’s security system, still partially active, detects the beakmen intrusion and activates a dormant **time-displaced T-Rex** (or another prehistoric creature) stored in stasis. The T-Rex bursts from the ice, attacking the beakmen scouts near the hideaway entrance.   
- **Alerting the Players:** The battle between the T-Rex and beakmen is loud and violent, alerting the players inside the hideaway. They can hear the roars, screams, and crashing ice. If they investigate, they’ll see the T-Rex rampaging and the beakmen struggling to defend themselves.   
- **Opportunity:** The players can use the chaos to slip past the distracted beakmen guards or even join the fray to turn the tide.   
 --- 
   
### 3. Reuniting the Party   
### Players Outside the Hideaway:   
- **Urgency:** The players outside see the obelisk land and the riders deploy. They realize the obelisk is a threat and need to regroup with the others quickly.   
- **Path to the Hideaway:** They can use the distraction of the T-Rex battle to sneak back to the hideaway entrance or fight their way through the scattered beakmen scouts.   
   
### Players Inside the Hideaway:   
- **Escape Plan:** The T-Rex battle creates an opening for them to escape the hideaway. They can either:   
    - Fight their way out alongside the T-Rex (risky but epic).   
    - Use Null-Eleven’s tech (e.g., a cloaking device or teleportation pad) to bypass the beakmen.   
    - Collapse part of the hideaway to block the beakmen and create a new exit.   
 --- 
   
### 4. The Obelisk’s Open Entrance   
- **Small Guard:** The obelisk’s entrance is lightly guarded—only 2-3 beakmen remain, focused on the T-Rex battle or communicating with the scouts.   
- **Distraction Tactics:** The players can:   
    - Create a diversion (e.g., using Null-Eleven’s gadgets, setting off an explosion, or luring the T-Rex closer).   
    - Sneak in using stealth or illusions.   
    - Bluff their way in by pretending to be scouts returning with information.   
 --- 
   
### 5. Time Pressure   
- **6-Hour Countdown:** The players know the scouts will return in 6 hours with reinforcements or worse—perhaps a larger force or a powerful beakmen commander. This creates a sense of urgency to:   
    - Sabotage the obelisk from within.   
    - Steal or destroy its power source.   
    - Prepare an ambush for the returning scouts.   
 --- 
   
### 6. Thematic Tie-Ins   
- **Null-Eleven’s Legacy:** The T-Rex is a remnant of his time-theft experiments, and the obelisk’s technology might be a corrupted version of his own designs. Clues in the hideaway could reveal how to disable the obelisk.   
- **Moral Choice:** The players could choose to:   
    - Destroy the obelisk, ending the threat but losing potential power.   
    - Attempt to control it, risking corruption or attracting Null-Eleven’s former enemies.   
 --- 
   
### 7. Closing the Scene   
- **Aftermath:** Once the players secure the obelisk (or fail to), the returning scouts arrive, leading to a climactic battle or a desperate escape.   
- **Rewards:** The players might gain access to Null-Eleven’s time-tech, a map to other hidden obelisks, or the gratitude of the nearby village.
