# Junis Challenge   
### Juni’s Challenge: The Squirrel’s Lesson   
\*\*Theme:\*\**The Cost of Friendship and Trust*   
 --- 
### Description for Players:   
As Juni presses her palm to the gate, the runes flare with a soft, golden light. The world dissolves, and she finds herself standing in the heart of a dense forest. The air is thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, and the distant chatter of squirrels fills the silence. Juni looks down and sees a tiny, trembling squirrel at her feet. Its eyes are wide with fear, and its voice is unmistakable:   
*"Don’t eat me!"* it squeaks.   
Juni recognizes that voice instantly. It’s **Dong**, transformed and terrified. She laughs, her voice warm and reassuring. *"I’m not going to eat you, you big oaf."* She reaches down to scoop him up, but the world shifts around her.   
 --- 
 --- 
### The Vision Unfolds:   
Suddenly, Juni is no longer herself—she is a **massive bear**, towering over the forest floor. Dong lies before her, no longer a squirrel, but wounded and bleeding in the mud. His voice is weak, but his eyes are filled with trust. *"You saved me,"* he says. *"Back in the forest. You spared me. Why?"*   
Juni feels the weight of his question. She remembers that day—the day she chose to spare him, to see the person beneath the brute. But the forest around them darkens, and a voice whispers through the trees: *"You love your friends, Juni. But love is a risk. What happens when they need more than you can give?"*   
The vision shifts again. Dong is bleeding out, his wounds too grave for her to heal. The voice echoes in her mind: *"You can save him. But only if you give up the bear. Forever."*   
The runes on the gate burn with a painful truth: *"You want to be the bear. But the bear cannot save them. Only Juni can."*   
 --- 
 --- 
### The Challenge:   
To open the gate, Juni must **choose**: she can either embrace her humanity to save Dong (and risk losing her connection to the bear) or accept that as a bear, she cannot always protect those she loves.   
Juni must kneel before the gate and speak her choice aloud:
*"I choose to save him. I am Juni. And I am the bear. But I won’t let my friends suffer for it."*   
 --- 
 --- 
### Mechanics:   
- **Defy Danger (CHA):** Roll +CHA to make the choice.   
    - **10+:** Juni accepts that she is both the girl *and* the bear—and that her humanity is what allows her to help her friends. The gate creaks open, but the citadel now tests her compassion. Every time she prioritizes her own desires over her friends, the citadel reacts—paths shift, guardians appear, and the whispers of the forest grow louder, reminding her of her responsibility.   
    - **7-9:** Juni hesitates. The gate opens, but she is left with doubt. She must perform an act of selflessness before the end of the session, or the citadel will force her to choose between her friends and her nature. The corridors grow colder, and the shadows seem to watch her, waiting for her to falter.   
    - **Miss:** Juni chooses the bear. Dong fades from the vision, and she rejoins the party as a bear—but every time she shifts into human form, she must **Defy Danger (WIS)** or lose the ability to shift back. The citadel’s guardians now see her as a threat to her friends, and they will act accordingly.   
   
### Why This Works for Juni’s Arc:   
- **Emotional Growth:** This challenge forces Juni to confront the **cost of her duality**—her love for the wild and her love for her friends. It pushes her to embrace the idea that she doesn’t have to choose between them, but she *does* have to accept the responsibilities that come with both.   
- **Consequences:** The trial doesn’t just open a gate—it changes how the citadel interacts with Juni. If she prioritizes herself over her friends, the citadel will reflect that back at her, reinforcing the idea that her choices have weight.   
- **Future Hooks:** The **Gem of Truth-Seeing** ties directly into Juni’s ongoing story. If she takes it, it could reveal deeper truths about her relationships with the party, her family, and herself. If she leaves it, she may continue to struggle with balancing her desires, but she will also remain free to define her own path.
