Conditions with Escalations   
1. What is their role in the narrative, and what do they
look like? Write as much or as little of this down as their Description.   
2. (Optional) What do they look like? How big are they?
What is a defining characteristic or two of their look?
If they are a person, how do they style themselves? Any notable ornaments, tattoos, jewelry, or scars? Write as much or as little of this down as their Appearance.   
3. What core goal or desire do they protect or advance?
Write that down as their Drive.   
4. What do they use to pursue their Drive? These could be approaches, tools, methods, weapons, or other things. Write these down as their Resources.   
5. For a dragon this could be teeth, claws, and fire. A
merchant might use an always-friendly face, an eye for appraisal, and connections with everyone   
6. (Optional) What are they particularly defended against or prepared for? Write this down as their Resistances.    
7. A knight with a tower shield might resist arrows, while a master ranger might resist someone sneaking up on them in the wilderness.   
8. (Optional) What weaknesses, desires, or secrets might they have that may or may not be true? Write these down as Rumors. These are potential ways to add an appropriate twist when someone Examines, Reads Someone, or Recalls something about them.   
9. What dramatic things might they do when desperate?
Write these down as their Escalations. Create 0-1 if
they are a minor obstacle, 2-3 if they are a significant
challenge, and 4-5 if they are a monumental power.
