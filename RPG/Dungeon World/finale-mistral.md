# Finale - Mistral   
Here’s a structured breakdown of your **Dungeon World** campaign’s **main themes**, **open storylines**, and **thrilling conclusions**—all while weaving in character backgrounds and unresolved threads for a climactic finale.   
 --- 
## 1. Main Themes   
Your campaign is rich with interconnected themes:   
- **Power & Corruption**: Soulstones, demonic pacts, and the abuse of magic (e.g., Uforn’s transformation, Zurboh’s imprisonment, the Wizard’s College).   
- **Identity & Deception**: Doppelgängers, vampires, and shapeshifters (e.g., Uforn vs. Demon-Uforn, Stanislaus, Gwendlewine’s return).   
- **Freedom & Trapping**: Characters are trapped by guilds, curses, or literal prisons (e.g., Rook’s debt to the Scoun, Flabistus in the flagstone, Zurboh in Hell).   
- **Loyalty & Betrayal**: Friendships tested by secrets and past actions (e.g., Dong’s betrayal by Grok, Rook’s love for Gwendlewine, Trixie’s family debt).   
- **Nature vs. Unnatural**: The forest’s corruption, rabid oxen, and the balance between druidic and demonic forces.   
 --- 
   
## 2. Open Storylines   
### A. Primary Quests   
1. **Free Zurboh from Hell**   
    - *How*: Use the Gem of Truth-Seeing, a tethered soulstone, and demon costumes to travel to Hell.   
    - *Why*: Zurboh is a key ally against the demonic Uforn and can reveal the truth about the King’s corruption.   
2. **Defeat Demon-Uforn**   
    - *How*: Trap him in a soulstone, expose his true name, or use Zurboh’s knowledge to banish him.   
    - *Why*: He’s consolidating power in Farfall, manipulating the King, and summoning demons.   
3. **Resolve the Greenvale Oxen Crisis**   
    - *How*: Destroy the flagstone (freeing Flabistus), shut down the mine, or confront the Vampire Stanislaus.   
    - *Why*: The oxen are a tool of demonic influence, and the mine is a source of soulstones.   
4. **Rook’s Freedom from the Scoun**   
    - *How*: Deliver the Gem of Truth-Seeing to the Scoun, or expose their corruption to the King.   
    - *Why*: Rook’s past is a liability, and the Scoun’s reach extends to Munstria and Farfall.   
   
### B. Secondary Threads   
- **Prince Darius’s Fate**: Is he turning into a vampire? Can he be saved or used as an ally?   
- **Gwendlewine’s Return**: Will Rook reunite with her, or is she a pawn of the Scoun?   
- **Dong’s Axe & Legacy**: Repairing his heirloom axe could symbolize reclaiming his identity.   
- **The Witch of Whapum**: Can she provide more answers about Zurboh or the demonic conspiracy?   
- **The Dragon’s Contract**: Arctus’s deal with the Wizards—could he be an ally against the College?   
 --- 
   
## 3. Thrilling Conclusions   
### Option 1: The Hellish Gambit   
**Climax**: The party uses the Gem of Truth-Seeing and a tethered soulstone to travel to Hell, free Zurboh, and confront Demon-Uforn.   
- **Character Arcs**:   
    - **Juni**: Uses her druidic magic to navigate Hell’s landscapes, bargaining with Flabistus (now freed) for safe passage.   
    - **Dong**: Faces his past (Grok’s betrayal) in a hallucination, reclaiming his axe’s power to break Zurboh’s chains.   
    - **Trixie**: Steals a demon’s true name from Hell’s archives, giving the party leverage.   
    - **Rook**: Sacrifices the Gem of Truth-Seeing to trick the Scoun, freeing himself from their grasp.   
- **Twist**: Demon-Uforn offers Rook a deal—betray the party for Gwendlewine’s freedom. Rook refuses, and the party banishes Uforn using Zurboh’s knowledge.   
- **Epilogue**: Zurboh becomes the new High Wizard, the Scoun is exposed, and the King rewards the party. Greenvale’s oxen crisis ends as Flabistus seals the flagstone.   
   
### Option 2: The Vampire’s Bargain   
**Climax**: The party allies with Stanislaus (the Vampire) to overthrow Demon-Uforn, using the soulstones as leverage.   
- **Character Arcs**:   
    - **Juni**: Negotiates with Stanislaus, offering him freedom from the mine in exchange for his help.   
    - **Dong**: Uses the Violation Glaive to disrupt Uforn’s demonic form, revealing his true name.   
    - **Trixie**: Uses the Silver Dagger to weaken Uforn, but it corrupts her—she must resist its power.   
    - **Rook**: Steals Uforn’s true name from the Wizard’s College archives, but the Scoun ambushes him. Gwendlewine intervenes, redeeming herself.   
- **Twist**: Stanislaus betrays the party, but Juni’s bond with Flabistus saves them—Flabistus traps Stanislaus in the flagstone.   
- **Epilogue**: The party exposes Uforn’s demonic form to the King, who executes him. The Scoun collapses, and Rook is free. Greenvale’s forest begins to heal.   
   
### Option 3: The Dragon’s Revenge   
**Climax**: The party returns to Arctus, offering to help him break his contract with the Wizards in exchange for his aid against Uforn.   
- **Character Arcs**:   
    - **Juni**: Convinces Arctus to spare the Wizards if they help free Zurboh.   
    - **Dong**: Uses the Blue-Crystal-Rod to amplify Arctus’s fire, destroying Uforn’s soulstone collection.   
    - **Trixie**: Steals the contract from the College, but triggers a trap—Arctus must choose between revenge and honor.   
    - **Rook**: Uses the Flask of Wind to escape the College’s collapse, but loses the Gem of Truth-Seeing in the chaos.   
- **Twist**: Arctus spares the Wizards but demands the mine’s closure. The party uses the soulstones to trap Uforn, but the College’s dragon awakes—Arctus fights it, buying the party time to escape.   
- **Epilogue**: The mine is sealed, the Scoun is exposed, and the King rewards the party. Rook is pardoned, and Dong’s axe is repaired by Arctus’s fire.   
 --- 
   
## 4. Weaving in Character Backgrounds   
- **Dong’s Redemption**: His flashback reveals Grok’s betrayal. Have Grok appear as a minion of Uforn, forcing Dong to confront him.   
- **Rook’s Love**: Gwendlewine could be a double agent—either helping the Scoun or secretly working against them.   
- **Trixie’s Family**: The rabid oxen crisis ties to her hometown. Resolving it could save her family’s farm.   
- **Juni’s Druidic Duty**: Freeing Flabistus and healing the forest fulfills her goal of living in harmony with nature.   
 --- 
   
## 5. Suggested 3-Hour Session Flow   
1. **Act 1: The Plan (30 min)**   
    - Recap open threads. Let the party choose: Hell, Vampire, or Dragon.   
    - Roleplay character motivations (e.g., Rook’s love, Dong’s axe, Trixie’s family).   
2. **Act 2: The Climax (90 min)**   
    - Execute the chosen plan (e.g., summoning Zurboh, fighting Uforn, or bargaining with Arctus).   
    - Introduce twists (betrayals, sacrifices, or unexpected allies).   
3. **Act 3: The Resolution (60 min)**   
    - Wrap up loose ends (Scoun, Greenvale, King’s reward).   
    - Epilogue: Where do the characters go next? (e.g., Rook reunites with Gwendlewine, Dong returns to his tribe, Juni becomes a forest guardian).   
 --- 
   
## 6. Final Tips   
- **Use the Apocalypse Engine**: Lean into partial successes—let the players’ failures escalate the stakes (e.g., a soulstone cracks, releasing a minor demon).   
- **Call Back to Early Moments**: Reference the “don’t eat me” mishap or the first cutpurse attempt for humor or drama.   
- **End on a High Note**: Leave one thread open for a potential sequel (e.g., “As the party celebrates, a raven delivers a message from the Witch of Whapum…”).
