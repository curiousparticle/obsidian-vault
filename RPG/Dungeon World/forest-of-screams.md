# Forest of Screams   
### Challenge 2: The Forest of Screams   
Location: A grove of petrified souls, their faces frozen in agony. The trees whisper secrets and lies, trying to lure the players deeper into the forest.
Mechanics:   
- The players must navigate the forest without touching the trees (touching a tree causes it to "awaken" and attack).   
- The trees whisper temptations:   
- Juni can communicate with the trees, but doing so risks hearing a truth that breaks her spirit (e.g., "Your friends will die here, and it will be your fault.").
Twist:   
- The forest is a living maze. The path shifts when they aren’t looking, forcing them to rely on landmarks or magic to find their way.   
   
### Enhanced Description: The Forest of Screams   
 --- 
**As you step into the grove, the air thickens with the weight of unseen sorrow.** The trees here are not like those of the mortal world—their bark is smooth as polished bone, their branches twisted into the shapes of screaming faces, mouths frozen mid-scream, eyes wide with eternal terror. The ground beneath your feet is soft, too soft, as if the earth itself is sighing with every step you take.   
A whisper slithers through the air, not from the wind, but from the trees themselves. They murmur in voices that sound like your own doubts, your deepest fears given tongue. *"Turn back,"* one hisses. *"You are not strong enough,"* another croons. The words wrap around your mind, tugging at your resolve, promising secrets if only you’d reach out and touch their cursed bark.   
But the forest is not just a place of whispers—it is alive. The path you walked moments ago is gone, swallowed by the shifting gloom. The trees lean in, their branches creaking like old bones, rearranging themselves when you blink. Landmarks flicker in and out of existence—a gnarled root one moment, a crumbling statue the next—only to vanish when you turn your back.   
And then there are the screams. Not from the trees, but from the earth itself, as if the forest is echoing the agony of those who came before you. Somewhere in this labyrinth, there is a way out. But the forest does not want to let you go.

### Navigating the Maze: Solutions Using Dungeon World Rules   
To escape the Forest of Screams, the players must rely on **wits, willpower, and the rules of Dungeon World** to guide them. Here are some clear, mechanics-driven solutions:   
 --- 
### 1. Discern Realities (WIS)   
- **Move:***"When you closely study a situation or person, what do you learn?"*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - The player notices that the trees only shift when no one is looking directly at them.   
    - They spot a faint, unnatural glow emanating from a specific landmark (a stone, a patch of mushrooms, a broken sword) that remains constant.   
    - They realize the whispers are loudest near the "false" paths, and quieter near the true one.   
- **Mechanics:** On a 10+, the GM reveals a useful clue. On a 7-9, the clue is obscured by a new danger or complication (e.g., a tree awakens nearby).   
 --- 
   
### 2. Defy Danger (WIS or CHA)   
- **Move:***"When you act despite an imminent threat, roll +WIS (to resist the forest’s illusions) or +CHA (to rally your party and keep them focused)."*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - A player resists the urge to touch a whispering tree, even as it promises answers.   
    - The party agrees to ignore the shifting paths and follows a single, unwavering direction (e.g., always keeping the sun to their left).   
    - Juni uses her connection to the trees to demand silence, but risks hearing a shattering truth (see below).   
- **Mechanics:** On a 10+, they resist the forest’s influence. On a 7-9, they succeed, but the forest retaliates (e.g., a tree awakens, or the path shifts again).   
 --- 
   
### 3. Juni’s Risky Communication (Special)   
- **Move:***"When you commune with the trees, roll +CHA. On a 10+, you learn a useful secret. On a 7-9, the trees reveal a truth that shakes you to your core (e.g., ‘Your friends will die here, and it will be your fault.’). On a miss, the forest claims a piece of your spirit—lose 1d4 WIS permanently."*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - The trees reveal the location of a hidden path or a weakness in the maze.   
    - The party must rally to support Juni after a harrowing revelation, or risk her falling into despair.   
 --- 
   
### 4. Spout Lore (INT)   
- **Move:***"When you recall a relevant fact about cursed forests or labyrinths, what do you remember?"*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - The player remembers that labyrinths often have a "heart"—a central clearing where the magic is strongest. Finding it might break the illusion.   
    - They recall that iron or cold-forged steel can disrupt fey magic, and use a weapon or tool to carve a mark into a tree, fixing a path.   
- **Mechanics:** On a 10+, the GM confirms the fact and suggests a course of action. On a 7-9, the information is partial or comes with a cost (e.g., the forest reacts violently).   
 --- 
   
### 5. Hack and Slash (STR) or Volley (DEX)   
- **Move:***"When you attack a tree to force a path, roll +STR (melee) or +DEX (ranged)."*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - A tree is felled, creating a temporary opening—but the forest screams in pain, and more trees awaken.   
    - The party must move quickly before the path closes again.   
- **Mechanics:** On a 10+, they clear a path. On a 7-9, they succeed, but the forest’s wrath is aroused (e.g., the ground becomes treacherous, or the whispers grow louder).   
 --- 
   
### 6. Use a Spell or Magic Item   
- **Move:***"When you unleash a spell or use a magic item to alter the forest, what happens?"*   
- **Possible Outcomes:**   
    - A *Light* spell reveals invisible threads connecting the trees, showing the "true" path.   
    - A *Detect Magic* spell highlights the most powerful illusion, allowing the party to ignore it.   
    - A *Protection from Evil* spell shields the party from the whispers for a short time.   
- **Mechanics:** Follow the spell’s rules, but the forest may adapt (e.g., illusions become more vivid, or new dangers appear).
