# Trixies Challenge   
### Trixie’s Vision in the Gates: The Truth of the Farm   
 --- 
As Trixie presses her palm to the gate, the runes flare with a cold, eerie light. The world dissolves, and she is no longer in the Iron Citadel—she is back in **Greenvale**, standing in the muddy fields of her family’s farm. The air is thick with the scent of damp earth and despair, but something is different. The memories are not as she remembers them.   
 --- 
### The Memory Unfolds   
Trixie sees herself as a younger girl, her hands clenched in frustration as she watches her mother weep over the meager harvest. The fields are barren, the crops stunted—not just for her family, but for *everyone* in Greenvale. The bad harvest wasn’t just their misfortune; it was a blight that struck the entire valley.   
- **Gorman’s Offer:** The vision shifts. Trixie sees **Gorman Whisperhands**, the landowner, standing in the doorway of their hut. He is not the cruel taskmaster she remembers. His voice is weary, his expression resigned. *"I can’t lower the rent again, Trixie. I’ve got my own family to feed. But I’ll give you work in the house—extra coin, extra food. It’s the best I can do."* Trixie’s younger self scowls. *"It’s not enough. We need more."* Gorman sighs. *"I know. But this is all I’ve got."*   
- **The Thefts:** The vision darkens. Trixie watches herself sneak into Gorman’s storeroom, her heart pounding with righteous anger. *"He’s hoarding food while we starve,"* she tells herself. But as she rummages through the sacks, she sees the truth: the storeroom is nearly empty. Gorman wasn’t hoarding—he was struggling, just like everyone else. The bad harvest hit him, too.   
- **The Confrontation:** The vision shifts again. Trixie is older now, standing in the mud outside the farmhouse, her voice raised in anger. *"You’re a thief, Gorman! You’re bleeding us dry!"* Gorman’s face twists—not with malice, but with frustration. *"I’ve given you chance after chance, Trixie. I’ve bent over backward for your family. But you won’t take help. You just take, and take, and take."* The words hit her like a slap. The runes on the gate burn with the truth: *"You wanted a villain. So you made one."*   
- **The Leaving:** The final memory is the worst. Trixie sees herself storming out of Greenvale, her pockets stuffed with coins she stole from Gorman’s strongbox. Her mother is crying, not because Trixie is leaving, but because she knows the truth: *"You didn’t do this for us, Trixie. You did it for you."*   
 --- 
   
### The Challenge   
To open the gate, Trixie must **acknowledge the truth**: that she wasn’t a hero stealing from a monster. She was a girl who wanted an excuse to leave, who needed someone to blame. The runes demand honesty. Trixie must kneel before the gate and speak the words aloud:   
*"I made Gorman the villain. I pushed him until he had no choice but to push back. And I left my family because I wanted to—not because I had to."*   
 --- 
 --- 
### Trixie’s Special Challenge: The Weight of Blame   
### Mechanics: Facing the Consequences   
- If Trixie admits the truth, the gate creaks open—but the citadel *remembers*. As the party enters, Trixie is pulled into a **vision of Greenvale**, not as it was, but as it *could have been*. The fields are lush, the harvests bountiful. Her family is gathered around a table, laughing, their bellies full. And Gorman is there, not as an enemy, but as a guest.   
- **The Trial:** Trixie must navigate a series of challenges that force her to confront the consequences of her choices:   
    1. **The Family’s Judgment:** Trixie is invisible to her family, a ghost in their midst. They speak of her not with anger, but with **sadness**. *"Trixie could have stayed,"* her mother says. *"She didn’t have to leave."* Trixie must listen to their words without interruption, without excuse.   
        - **Defy Danger (WIS):** Roll +WIS to swallow her pride and accept their judgment.   
            - **10+:** Trixie gains insight into the true weight of her actions. Her mother turns and looks *right at her*, as if she can see her. *"It’s not too late to come home,"* she says. The vision shifts, and Trixie rejoins the party—but the citadel’s guardians now test her honesty. Every time she lies or shifts blame, the citadel reacts (e.g., doors seal, flames intensify).   
            - **7-9:** Trixie struggles to accept their words. The vision lingers, and the citadel’s corridors shift to reflect her inner turmoil. She must perform an act of honesty before the end of the session, or the citadel will turn against her.   
            - **Miss:** Trixie’s pride gets the better of her. She lashes out, and the family’s sadness turns to anger. The vision becomes a nightmare, and Trixie must **fight her way out**—but every strike against her family’s spectral forms deals damage to her own spirit (lose 1d4 CHA permanently).   
    2. **The Offer:** The vision shifts again. Gorman appears, not as the monster she remembers, but as a tired man. *"You wanted to leave, Trixie. I get that. But you didn’t have to burn the bridge."* He offers her a choice:   
        - **Apologize and accept his help** (lose 1d4 CHA, but the vision ends, and Trixie rejoins the party with a newfound sense of humility).   
        - **Refuse and face the consequences** (the citadel’s guardians now target Trixie’s dishonesty, forcing her to rely on her allies to survive).   
    3. **The Lesson:** If Trixie accepts the truth, the vision shifts one final time. She sees Greenvale not as it was, but as it *could be*—a place where she is welcomed home, where her family forgives her, where Gorman is not a villain, but a neighbor. The runes on the gate whisper: *"The past can’t be changed. But the future can."*   
 --- 
   
### Twist: The Gem of Truth-Seeing   
- If Trixie succeeds in her trial, her mother reveals a secret: *"The Gem of Truth-Seeing is hidden in this citadel. Take it, Trixie. See the truth of what you’ve done—and what you could still do."* This sets up a **moral choice** for Trixie: Does she take the gem to finally understand the depth of her actions, knowing it may force her to return to Greenvale and face her past? Or does she leave it, and let her old grievances stand?   
 --- 
 --- 
   
### Why This Works for Trixie’s Arc   
- **Emotional Growth:** Trixie’s challenge forces her to confront her own role in her family’s struggles and her departure from Greenvale. This is a pivotal moment for her character, especially given her self-image as a rebel and a thief "fighting the system."   
- **Consequences:** The trial doesn’t just open a gate—it changes how the citadel interacts with Trixie, reinforcing the theme that her choices have weight and that honesty is its own kind of strength.   
- **Future Hooks:** The Gem of Truth-Seeing ties directly into Trixie’s ongoing story, giving you a powerful tool to challenge her (and the party) later in the campaign.
