# In Hell   
Here’s a **multi-layered set of challenges** for your players as they navigate **Hell** in search of **Zurboh**. Each challenge is designed to test their wits, resolve, and teamwork, while immersing them in the nightmarish landscape of Hell. The goal: **find and free Zurboh** before Hell’s horrors consume them.   
 --- 
### Setting the Scene: Hell’s Landscape   
Hell is a **shifting, labyrinthine realm** of fire, shadow, and torment. The air is thick with the scent of burning flesh and sulfur, and the ground is a mix of jagged obsidian, rivers of molten lava, and fields of screaming souls. The sky is a **swirling void**, occasionally lit by flashes of crimson lightning.   
The players arrive in a **desolate plain**, where the ground is cracked and **blood seeps from the earth**. In the distance, they can see:   
- A **fortress of black iron and bone** (likely where Zurboh is imprisoned).   
- A **river of fire** with a rickety bridge.   
- A **forest of petrified screams** (trees made of twisted, frozen souls).   
- A **chasm** where something **massive** stirs below.   
   
A **ghostly whisper** echoes in their minds—**Zurboh’s voice**, weak but urgent:   
> "Find me... before they break me. The Iron Citadel... but beware the trials. Hell will test you."   

 --- 
## Challenge 1: The Bridge of Lies   
**Location:** A narrow bridge spans a river of fire, leading toward the Iron Citadel. The bridge is made of **shifting, semi-transparent stones** that only appear solid when stepped on with **absolute conviction**.   
**Mechanics:**   
- Each player must **answer a question truthfully** to cross safely. If they lie or hesitate, the stone crumbles, and they fall into the river (taking damage and requiring a rescue).   
- The questions are **personal and tailored** to their characters:   
    - **Rook:***"What do you regret more: leaving the Scoun, or leaving Gwendlewine?"*   
    - **Trixie:***"Would you betray your family to save yourself?"*   
    - **Dong:***"Do you hate Grok more than you love your tribe?"*   
    - **Juni:***"If you could live as a bear forever, would you give up your humanity?"*   
   
**Twist:**   
- Halfway across, a **shadowy figure** (a lesser demon) appears, offering to carry them across—for a price. *"I’ll take a memory. Just one. The happiest one you have."*   
 --- 
   
## Challenge 2: The Forest of Screams   
**Location:** A grove of **petrified souls**, their faces frozen in agony. The trees whisper secrets and lies, trying to lure the players deeper into the forest.   
**Mechanics:**   
- The players must **navigate the forest without touching the trees** (touching a tree causes it to "awaken" and attack).   
- The trees **whisper temptations**:   
    - *"Turn back. Zurboh is already lost."*   
    - *"Your friends will betray you. Leave them behind."*   
    - *"There’s a shortcut... if you’re willing to pay."*   
- **Juni** can communicate with the trees, but doing so risks **hearing a truth that breaks her spirit** (e.g., *"Your friends will die here, and it will be your fault."*).   
   
**Twist:**   
- The forest is a **living maze**. The path shifts when they aren’t looking, forcing them to rely on **landmarks or magic** to find their way.   
 --- 
   
## Challenge 3: The Chasm of Despair   
**Location:** A bottomless chasm blocks their path. A **chain bridge** sways precariously over the abyss, but it’s guarded by a **hulking demon** with a whip of barbed wire.   
**Mechanics:**   
- The demon demands a **toll**: *"One of you must stay behind. Forever."*   
- If they refuse, the demon attacks, using its whip to **disarm or trip** them.   
- If they comply, the demon **drags the "volunteer" into the chasm** (but it’s a trick—they’re actually teleported to a **prison cell** in the Iron Citadel).   
   
**Twist:**   
- The chasm is **home to a forgotten god**, who whispers promises of power to anyone who listens. *"Jump, and I will make you a king."*   
 --- 
   
## Challenge 4: The Iron Citadel   
**Location:** A fortress of **black iron and bone**, its gates sealed with **runes of binding**. Inside, Zurboh is imprisoned in a **cage of living flame**, suspended over a pit of screaming souls.   
**Mechanics:**   
- **The Gates:** The runes require a **drop of blood from someone who has betrayed a friend** to open. (Rook’s past with the Scoun or Dong’s history with Grok could work.)   
- **The Guardians:** Inside, they face:   
    - **The Warden**: A towering demon with a **mirror shield** that reflects their worst fears.   
    - **The Soul Eater**: A floating, emaciated horror that **drains HP with every lie spoken**.   
- **Zurboh’s Cage:** The cage is locked with a **puzzle**:   
    - *"Speak my true name, and the flames will die."*   
    - *"Break the cage, and the prison collapses."*   
    - *"Offer a soul in exchange, and I will walk free."*   
   
**Twist:**   
- Zurboh is **not alone**. Uforn’s **demonic doppelgänger** is there, torturing him. *"Ah, more fools to feed the flames."*   
 --- 
   
## Challenge 5: The Final Bargain   
**Location:** The heart of the citadel, where a **massive obsidian throne** sits empty. A **contract** floats in the air, written in blood.   
**Mechanics:**   
- The contract offers Zurboh’s freedom—but at a cost:   
    - *"Sign, and one of you will take Zurboh’s place."*   
    - *"Sign, and Hell gains a foothold in your world."*   
    - *"Sign, and a secret you’ve kept will be revealed to your worst enemy."*   
- If they refuse, the citadel begins to **collapse**, and they must **fight their way out** while carrying Zurboh.   
   
**Twist:**   
- Zurboh **begs them not to sign**. *"Don’t damn yourselves for me. There’s another way."*   
 --- 
   
## Escape from Hell   
If they free Zurboh, they must **find their tether back to the mortal world**—but Hell doesn’t give up its prey easily:   
- **The River of Fire** is now a **tsunami of flames**.   
- **The Bridge of Lies** is crumbling.   
- **The Forest of Screams** is **alive and hunting** them.   
- **The Chasm** has become a **portal to something worse**.   
   
**Final Test:**   
- They must **choose**:   
    - **Run**, and risk losing someone in the chaos.   
    - **Fight**, and hold off Hell’s horrors long enough to escape.   
    - **Bargain**, and offer Hell something it wants (a memory, a name, a future favor).   
 --- 
   
## Epilogue: The Cost of Freedom   
If they escape:   
- Zurboh is **weak but grateful**. *"You’ve done what no one else could. But Hell remembers you now... and it will want revenge."*   
- Each player **gains a "mark"** from Hell—a scar, a whisper in their dreams, or a shadow that follows them.   
- **Rook’s Gem of Truth-Seeing** is now **cracked**, its magic unstable.   
- **Dong’s axe** glows with **Hellfire**, a reminder of the deal they made.   
   
If they fail:   
- Zurboh is **lost forever**, and Hell’s influence **seeps into their world**.   
- **Uforn’s demonic doppelgänger** returns, **more powerful than ever**.   
- One of the players is **left behind**, their soul trapped in Hell.   
 --- 
   
### GM Tips   
- **Time Pressure:** Use a **countdown** (e.g., "The citadel trembles—you have minutes before it collapses!").   
- **Moral Dilemmas:** Force players to **sacrifice something**—a memory, a possession, or a piece of their soul.   
- **Hell’s Rules:** Emphasize that **Hell twists everything**—promises are lies, shortcuts are traps, and help always comes with a price.   
 --- 
   
This set of challenges will push your players to their limits, forcing them to **confront their pasts, make impossible choices, and fight for their souls**. How will they navigate the horrors of Hell? Will they save Zurboh, or will Hell claim them all?
