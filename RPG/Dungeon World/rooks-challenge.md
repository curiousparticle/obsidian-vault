# Rooks Challenge   
### Revised Rook’s Challenge: The Ghosts of the Scoun   
 --- 
### Rook’s Vision in the Gates   
As Rook presses his hand to the gate, the runes flare, and the world dissolves. He is back in **Munstria**, standing in the shadow of the grand theater where he last saw **Gwendlwine**. But the vision twists, and suddenly he is in the **Scoun’s hideout**, the night everything changed.   
- **The Betrayal:** Rook sees himself standing over **Lynnmo and Gerald**, his closest friends in the Scoun. They are bound and gagged, their eyes wide with fear and betrayal. Rook’s voice is cold, distant: *"I’m leaving. And I’m taking the guild’s stash. If you tell anyone, I’ll make sure the Scoun knows it was you who let me go."* He leaves them there, knowing full well what the Scoun does to traitors. The vision shifts—Lynnmo and Gerald are dragged before the guildmaster, their fingers broken one by one. The gate’s runes sear the truth into Rook’s mind: *"You didn’t just leave them. You sacrificed them."*   
- **The Truth:** The gate shows Rook the consequences of his actions. Lynnmo and Gerald didn’t just betray him—they were *forced* to, because Rook gave the Scoun no other choice. The runes burn with the words: *"You were never the victim. You were the blade."*   
- **The Challenge:** To open the gate, Rook must **apologize to the ghosts of Lynnmo and Gerald**—not just in his mind, but aloud, in front of the party. The runes demand an admission of guilt, and the gate will only open if he speaks the words: *"I betrayed you. I left you to the Scoun’s mercy. And I’m sorry."*

### Rook’s Special Challenge: The Apology   
### Mechanics: Facing the Past   
- If Rook apologizes sincerely, the gate creaks open—but the citadel *remembers*. As the party enters, Rook is pulled into a **pocket of the past**, a ghostly recreation of the Scoun’s hideout. Lynnmo and Gerald are there, but they are not as he left them. They are whole, unbroken—and they are *angry*.   
- **The Trial:** Rook must face his former friends in a trial of words, not blades. To proceed, he must:   
    1. **Listen to their accusations** (e.g., *"You called us family, then left us to rot."*).   
    2. **Answer honestly** (e.g., *"I was afraid. I thought I had no choice."*).   
    3. **Offer amends** (e.g., *"If I could take it back, I would. But I can’t. All I can do now is try to be better."*).   
- **Possible Moves:**   
    - **Defy Danger (CHA):** Roll +CHA to steel himself and speak the truth, even if it hurts.   
        - **10+:** Lynnmo and Gerald nod, their anger fading. *"We forgive you,"* Lynnmo says. *"But don’t you dare forget us."* The pocket of the past dissolves, and Rook rejoins the party—but the citadel’s traps now test his resolve. Every time he acts selfishly, the citadel reacts (e.g., doors seal, flames intensify).   
        - **7-9:** The ghosts accept his apology, but they warn him: *"Words aren’t enough. Prove it."* Rook must perform an act of selflessness before the end of the session, or the citadel will turn against him.   
        - **Miss:** The ghosts turn on him, their anger manifesting as spectral blades. Rook must **fight his way out**, but every strike against them deals damage to his own soul (lose 1d4 WIS permanently).   
   
### Why This Works for Rook’s Arc   
- **Personal Growth:** Rook’s challenge forces him to confront his past actions and take responsibility for the pain he caused. This is a pivotal moment for his character, especially given his lingering feelings for Gwendlwine and his desire to "become someone better."   
- **Consequences:** The trial doesn’t just open a gate—it changes how the citadel interacts with Rook, reinforcing the theme that his choices have weight.   
- **Future Hooks:** The Gem of Truth-Seeing ties directly into Rook’s ongoing story with the Scoun, giving you a powerful tool to challenge him (and the party) later in the campaign.
