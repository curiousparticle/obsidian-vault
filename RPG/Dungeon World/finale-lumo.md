## 2️⃣ Three Thrilling Conclusions (Choose Your Adventure)   
Below each option includes **key scenes**, **required dice mechanics**, and **how each character shines**. Feel free to mix‑and‑match elements to fit the three‑hour window.   
### 🎭 Option A – “Seal the Rift, Save the Kingdom”   
**Goal:** Close the demonic portal, free Zurboh, and stop the oxen plague in one epic showdown.   
1. **Setup – The Ritual Site**   
    - The party meets at the **flagstone in the forest** (the source of the red‑eyed oxen).   
    - The flagstone is a *planar anchor*: if the party activates it with the **Blue‑Crystal‑Rod** (detects magical resonance) and the **Gem of Truth‑Seeing** (reveals hidden sigils), they can perform a *Contact Other Plane* ritual.   
2. **Key Conflict – Uforn’s Assault**   
    - As the ritual begins, **Uforn (demon)** erupts, flanked by **zombie‑oxen** (the rabid herd animated by demonic energy).   
    - **Combat:** Use “hard moves” (failed rolls) to trigger dramatic setbacks (e.g., the flagstone cracks, causing a temporary surge of fire).   
    - **Juni** can *Discern Reality* to locate the hidden rune that weakens Uforn; **Dong** can *smash* the cracked gem fragments to release a burst of holy fire; **Rook** can use the **Gem of Truth‑Seeing** to expose Uforn’s true name (if the DM allows a skill check), giving the party a *once‑only* “banishment” option; **Trixie** can slip in to steal a *silver dagger* from the demon’s grasp (extra damage to undead).   
3. **Climax – Closing the Rift**   
    - If the party successfully names Uforn *and* channels the rod’s energy, the rift collapses, **Zurboh is pulled back from Hell** (thanks to the tether created by the cracked Soul‑Stone).   
    - The **oxen revert to normal** as the demonic influence lifts.   
4. **Resolution**   
    - **King Theobold** rewards each player **30 gp** (the promised bounty) plus a **unique magical token** (e.g., a tiny soul‑stone that can store one spell).   
    - **Rook** hands over the Gem of Truth‑Seeing to the Scoun, earning a **truce** and a **safe‑house** in Farfall.   
    - **Juni** receives a **forest guardian title** from the druids, granting a permanent +1 to Nature checks.   
   
**Why it works:** It ties every loose thread together, gives each character a spotlight, and resolves the central “demon‑gem” mystery in a single, cinematic moment.   
 --- 
### ⚔️ Option B – “The Dragon’s Bargain & the Hell‑Tether”   
**Goal:** Make a pact with Arctus the dragon, use his treasure to forge a hell‑tether, and launch a daring raid on the demon‑infested mine.   
1. **Setup – Dragon Negotiation**   
    - The party returns to **Arctus** (the dragon) with the **contract** they retrieved.   
    - Arctus offers **two powerful items** (choose: a *flame‑kissed war‑axe* for Dong, a *cloak of shadows* for Rook, a *staff of wild growth* for Juni, a *bag of endless coins* for Trixie) **if** they retrieve a **“Heart‑Gem”** from the mine and **seal the demon** inside it.   
2. **The Mine Assault**   
    - The mine is now a **demon‑infested cavern** guarded by **Uforn’s minions** (imp‑like fire sprites, corrupted miners).   
    - **Combat:** Use the **Blue‑Crystal‑Rod** to locate the Heart‑Gem, the **Gem of Truth‑Seeing** to see hidden traps, and the **Silver Dagger** (Trixie’s loot) to bypass demonic wards.   
    - **Rook** can attempt a *Stealth* check to sneak past the Scoun’s goons who have also entered the mine for the gem.   
3. **Hell‑Tether Creation**   
    - Once the Heart‑Gem is secured, the party performs a **ritual** (using the cracked Soul‑Stone as a vessel) to bind **Uforn’s essence** inside the gem.   
    - **Success** = Uforn is trapped, the **portal to Hell closes**, and the **gem becomes a usable “tether”** for future planar travel (a cool future hook).   
4. **Final Twist – The King’s Choice**   
    - The King arrives with his troops, demanding the **Soul‑Stone** for his own power.   
    - The party can **refuse** (risking a showdown) or **hand it over** (gaining a royal favor but losing the tether).   
5. **Resolution**   
    - If they keep the tether, they earn **Dragon’s Favor** (+2 to intimidation or persuasion when dealing with draconic entities).   
    - **Rook** settles his debt by giving the Scoun a **false gem** (the truth‑seeing gem reveals the deception, but the guild respects the gesture).   
    - **Trixie** finally secures **wealth** (the bag of endless coins) and can buy a **family farm** for Greenvale.   
   
**Why it works:** It leans heavily on the **treasure‑hunt vibe** of Dungeon World, gives the dragon a meaningful role, and provides a clear “win‑or‑lose” decision that can be resolved quickly with a few skill checks.   
 --- 
### 🌌 Option C – “The Royal Coup & the Demon‑War”   
**Goal:** Turn the political tide by exposing the Deputy High Wizard’s treachery, rally the kingdom against the demonic incursion, and let the party become legends.   
1. **Setup – Gathering Evidence**   
    - Using the **Gem of Truth‑Seeing**, the party infiltrates the **King’s council chambers** (disguised as servants or scholars).   
    - They discover **documents** proving that **Uforn (the demon)** has been manipulating the **King’s advisors** and that the **oxen plague** is a side‑effect of a hidden **summoning circle** beneath Greenvale.   
2. **Public Reveal**   
    - The party stages a **public demonstration** in the town square of Farfall: they shatter the **Soul‑Stone** (partial crack) to release a controlled burst of demonic energy, then **bind** it with the **Blue‑Crystal‑Rod** and **silence** it with the **Silver Dagger**.   
    - The crowd witnesses the **true nature of the demon** and the **corruption** in the royal court.   
3. **Mass Mobilization**   
    - The **King**, embarrassed but grateful, appoints the party as **Royal Envoys** to lead a **combined force of soldiers, wizards, and druids** against the demon‑infested forest.   
    - **Juni** commands the druidic forces to cleanse the forest; **Dong** leads the frontline barbarian charge; **Rook** coordinates the thieves‑guild militia; **Trixie** handles logistics and supplies (using her stash of coins).   
4. **Epic Battle – The Demon’s Maw**   
    - The final encounter occurs at the **heart of the flagstone** where the demon is anchored.   
    - The party must **hold the line** while the **royal army** completes a massive *Seal* spell (requires a **minute** of uninterrupted chanting).   
    - **Critical moments:** If any character fails a roll, the demon gains a temporary boost (e.g., extra damage, summons minor demons).   
5. **Conclusion – Legends Born**   
    - With the demon sealed, the **oxen return to normal**, the **king’s reputation is restored**, and the party receives **titles** (e.g., “Guardian of Greenvale”, “Champion of Farfall”).   
    - **Rook** negotiates a **permanent truce** with the Scoun, gaining a **network of informants**.   
    - **Trixie** finally secures a **family estate** funded by the king’s gratitude.   
    - **Juni** is offered a **seat on the Druidic Council**; **Dong** is given a **legendary war‑axe** forged from the dragon’s hoard.   
   
**Why it works:** It turns the campaign into a *political epic*, letting the players feel the weight of their choices on an entire kingdom. It also gives a satisfying “heroic legend” ending that fits a three‑hour sprint—most of the heavy lifting is narrative exposition and a single, climactic combat round.

You can even **combine** the first 10‑minute “setup” from Option A (the flagstone) with the treasure payoff from Option B (dragon’s reward) and end with a short “royal decree” from Option C to give the party a lasting legacy.

## 4️⃣ How to Run It Smoothly (Tips for a 3‑Hour Session)   
1. **Prep 3 “scene cards”** – one for the ritual site, one for the dragon negotiation, one for the final showdown. Write the key NPC lines and the required rolls on the back.   
2. **Timer on the ritual** – give the party **5‑minute real‑time** to gather components; this creates tension without dragging.   
3. **Use “hard moves” sparingly** – pick 2–3 dramatic failures (e.g., flagstone cracks, demon surges) to keep the stakes high.   
4. **Reward with narrative tokens** – instead of tracking many coins, hand out **story tokens** (e.g., “You earned a royal title”, “You have a dragon’s favor”). These are quick to note and feel rewarding.   
5. **End with a “future hook”** – a whisper that the **Hell‑tether** still pulses, or a **new quest** from the Scoun, so the adventure can continue if you ever want to pick it up again.
