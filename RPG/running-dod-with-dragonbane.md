# Running DOD with Dragonbane   
Running *Deserts of Desolation*—a classic, sprawling adventure originally designed for *Basic D&D*—with the **Dragonbane** rule set is a bold and exciting idea! Here’s how you can approach it, along with some key considerations and tips to make it work smoothly:   
 --- 
### 1. Why Dragonbane is a Great Fit   
- **Fast-Paced, Deadly Combat**: Dragonbane’s brutal combat system will make the desert’s dangers (sandstorms, monsters, and rival factions) feel more visceral and high-stakes. Players will need to think tactically and avoid overconfidence, which fits the adventure’s survivalist tone.   
- **Streamlined Rules**: Dragonbane’s lightweight mechanics mean less bookkeeping and more focus on storytelling and exploration—perfect for a sandbox-style adventure like *Deserts of Desolation*.   
- **Flexible Magic**: The Willpower Point (WP) system for magic can add a unique twist to the adventure’s mystical elements (e.g., the cursed city of Alcazzar, genies, and desert magic).   
 --- 
   
### 2. Key Adaptations for Deserts of Desolation   
### A. Environmental Rules   
- **Heat and Thirst**: Use Dragonbane’s injury and fatigue rules to simulate dehydration and heat exhaustion. For example:   
    - **Daily Survival Rolls**: Require CON or WIL checks to avoid fatigue or injury from extreme heat.   
    - **Water Management**: Track water supplies strictly. Running out could mean automatic fatigue or even HP loss.   
- **Sandstorms**: Treat these as environmental hazards, requiring AGL or STR checks to avoid getting lost or buried. Use the Fear table for disorienting effects.   
   
### B. Factions and NPCs   
- *Deserts of Desolation* is rich with factions (slavers, nomads, genies, and the cursed city’s inhabitants). Use Dragonbane’s simple reaction rolls (e.g., CHA or WIL checks) to handle negotiations and alliances. The game’s “attitude” system (friendly/hostile/neutral) can help streamline interactions.   
   
### C. Monsters and Enemies   
- **Reskin Creatures**: Dragonbane’s bestiary is smaller than D&D’s, so reskin existing monsters (e.g., use “desert trolls” for giants, “sand serpents” for snakes) and adjust their stats slightly for balance.   
- **Use Fear and Injury Tables**: These will make encounters with creatures like mummies, genies, or desert predators feel more dynamic and memorable.   
   
### D. The Cursed City of Alcazzar   
- **Corruption and Madness**: Use Dragonbane’s injury or WIL checks to represent the city’s cursed effects. For example:   
    - Failed WIL checks could result in temporary madness or hallucinations.   
    - Prolonged exposure might require CON checks to avoid gaining permanent injuries or traits.   
 --- 
   
### 3. Pacing and Structure   
- **Modular Approach**: *Deserts of Desolation* is a sandbox. Break it into smaller, goal-oriented arcs (e.g., “Find the Oasis of the Moon,” “Negotiate with the Nomad Tribe,” “Survive the Sandstorm”). This keeps players focused and prevents overwhelm.   
- **Session Cliffhangers**: End each session with a clear hook (e.g., “As you camp for the night, the sand begins to shift unnaturally…”). This maintains momentum and gives you time to prep based on their choices.   
 --- 
   
### 4. Player Engagement Tips   
- **Collaborative Worldbuilding**: Ask players to tie their characters to the desert (e.g., a nomad background, a curse from Alcazzar). This builds investment in the setting.   
- **Use Optional Rules**: If players struggle with the lethality, consider tweaking HP or recovery rules—but keep the core tension intact.   
 --- 
   
### 5. Tools to Steal from Dragonbane   
- **Initiative Cards**: Use these for chaotic encounters (e.g., ambushes, sandstorms) to keep combat snappy.   
- **Travel Rules**: Dragonbane’s travel mechanics (e.g., random encounters, fatigue) can be adapted for desert journeys. Roll for events like mirages, oases, or bandit attacks.   
 --- 
   
### 6. Potential Challenges   
- **Lethality**: *Deserts of Desolation* is already deadly; Dragonbane amplifies this. Be prepared for character deaths and discuss tone expectations with your group upfront.   
- **Magic Limitations**: Dragonbane’s magic is less flashy than D&D’s. Emphasize the mystery and rarity of desert magic (e.g., genie wishes, cursed artifacts) to keep it feeling special.   
 --- 
   
### Final Thought   
*Deserts of Desolation* + *Dragonbane* is a fantastic combo if you want a gritty, survival-focused campaign with streamlined rules. Lean into the adventure’s themes of desperation, discovery, and curse—Dragonbane’s mechanics will heighten the tension and make every choice feel consequential.
