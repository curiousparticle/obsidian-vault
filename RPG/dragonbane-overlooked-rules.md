# Dragonbane Overlooked Rules   
Here are some of the most commonly forgotten or overlooked rules in Dragonbane:   
1. **Swimming Rules**: Many groups forget to apply the specific rules for swimming, which can be important in certain adventures or encounters involving water. These rules can significantly impact movement and combat in aquatic environments.   
2. **Fear Tables and Injuries**: The use of fear tables and the detailed injury system is often overlooked, but these mechanics add depth to combat and can make encounters more immersive and challenging
.   
3. **Armor Protection**: A golden rule is that armor protects against all physical damage (damage to hit points) unless an exception is stated. For example, metal armor does not protect against Lightning Bolt damage, but it does protect against Fire damage unless specified otherwise. This is a common point of confusion
.   
4. **Initiative Cards**: The use of numbered cards (1–10) for initiative is sometimes forgotten, especially by groups used to other systems. This can lead to confusion if not clarified at the start of combat
.   
5. **Action and Movement Clarifications**: Players sometimes try to save actions or movement for later in the turn, but the rules state you must perform your action and move on your turn—you cannot save them for later (except for the Waiting action)
.   
6. **Magic and Metal**: Mages cannot have metal weapons or armor at hand while casting spells, unless specified otherwise. This is a subtle but important restriction that is often missed
.   
7. **Spell Power Levels and Willpower Points (WP)**: The cost for increasing spell power levels is 2 WP per level up to level 3, totaling 6 WP. Magic Tricks always cost 1 WP. This is sometimes misremembered or overlooked, especially by new players
.   
8. **Optional Rules**: Some rules are marked as optional, and groups may forget to decide upfront whether to use them, leading to inconsistency during play
.
