# Africa Safari Packing Checklist   
**Alan Moreira · ZRH → JNB 29 June 2026**   
> Pack into two bags: a soft duffel (≤20 kg, max 30×42×70 cm) for the bush camp legs, and a main bag that stays at Pioneers Victoria Falls (5–13 July). Items marked 🛖 go in the duffel; everything else can go in the main bag.   

 --- 
## 🔑 Documents & Money   
*Carry-on only — never in checked luggage*   
- [x] US passport (valid to Jan 2027+)   
- [x] Zimbabwe e-visa printout with QR code   
- [x] Namibia e-visa printout with QR code   
- [ ] Zimbabwe arrival declaration printout   
- [x] SWISS e-ticket (booking YWQX63 / ticket 7242349282782)   
- [x] Aardvark itinerary + emergency contacts printout   
- [x] Travel insurance documents (with emergency numbers)   
- [x] Photocopies of passport (stored separately from original)   
- [x] At least 2 credit cards (Visa/Mastercard — Amex rarely accepted)   
- [ ] USD cash — small bills ($1, $5, $10, $20); avoid $100s   
- [ ] South African Rand (small denominations)   
- [ ] Namibian Dollars   
 --- 
## 👕 Clothing   
*Neutral/earthy tones only — no red, white, or bright colours on safari. Avoid strong blues (tsetse flies).*   
**Warm days (all destinations):**   
- [ ] 🛖 3–4 lightweight T-shirts (khaki, olive, brown, beige)   
- [ ] 🛖 2–3 long-sleeved lightweight cotton shirts (sun + mosquito protection)   
- [ ] 🛖 2 pairs of shorts   
- [ ] 🛖 2 pairs of long trousers (bush walks, cool evenings, tsetse fly areas)   
- [ ] Swimsuit   
- [ ] Sarong / cotton wrap-around   
**Cold mornings/evenings (July is winter — open vehicles get cold fast):**   
- [ ] 🛖 Fleece or warm sweater   
- [ ] 🛖 Light windproof/rain jacket   
- [ ] 🛖 Warm hat / beanie   
- [ ] 🛖 Scarf or neck snood   
- [ ] Thin gloves (optional but very welcome on 5am game drives)   
**Footwear:**   
- [ ] Walking shoes or sneakers (not bright white)   
- [ ] Sandals / flip flops   
- [ ] Lightweight waterproof hiking boots (optional — useful for Sossusvlei dune walks)   
**Other:**   
- [ ] 🛖 Underwear and socks (enough for the trip; laundry available at all camps)   
 --- 
## 💊 Health & Toiletries   
**Prescription (carry-on, with extra supply):**   
- [x] 🛖 Malaria prophylaxis — Malarone or Doxycycline (full course)   
- [ ] Any regular prescription medication + extra supply   
**Over-the-counter:**   
- [ ] 🛖 DEET insect repellent 25–35% (apply religiously at dusk and dawn)   
- [ ] 🛖 SPF 50+ sunscreen   
- [ ] 🛖 Wide-brimmed sun hat — non-negotiable   
- [ ] 🛖 Lip balm with SPF   
- [ ] Rehydration salts (dehydration mimics malaria — take seriously)   
- [ ] Imodium / anti-diarrheal   
- [ ] Antihistamine tablets + cream (tsetse bites, insect reactions)   
- [ ] Paracetamol / ibuprofen   
- [ ] Plasters + antiseptic cream   
- [ ] Blister plasters (Sossusvlei dune walks)   
- [ ] Motion sickness tablets (bumpy game drives + charter flights)   
- [ ] Small basic first aid kit   
- [ ] Hand sanitiser   
**Toiletries:**   
- [ ] All personal toiletries (shampoo + soap provided at lodges)   
 --- 
## 📷 Electronics & Safari Gear   
**Camera:**   
- [ ] Camera body (DSLR or mirrorless — capable of fast burst shooting)   
- [ ] 70–300mm telephoto lens (ideal for wildlife)   
- [ ] Wide/standard lens (18–80mm, optional second lens)   
- [ ] Extra batteries (charge at every opportunity)   
- [ ] Multiple memory cards   
- [ ] Blower, lens pen, lens cloths (dust is relentless)   
- [ ] Camera bag or protective case   
- [ ] ⛔ No drone — illegal in all national parks in Zimbabwe and Namibia   
**Safari essentials:**   
- [ ] 🛖 Binoculars — 8×40 or 10×40 (one per person; this is not optional)   
- [ ] 🛖 Reusable water bottle (camps discourage single-use plastic)   
- [ ] 🛖 Headlamp / small torch + spare batteries (camp paths are unlit at night)   
**Other electronics:**   
- [ ] 🛖 Power bank / portable charging pack   
- [ ] Worldwide plug adapter — Southern Africa uses **3-pin round plug** (same as UK round pins)   
- [ ] 🛖 Earphones   
- [ ] Kindle or books (some camps have no signal — you'll actually read)   
 --- 
## ❌ Do NOT Pack / Leave at Home   
- [ ] ~~Anything bright red, white, or strongly blue~~ (frightens wildlife / attracts tsetse)   
- [ ] ~~Plastic bags~~ — banned in Namibia national parks; ditch duty-free bags on arrival in Windhoek   
- [ ] ~~Drone~~ — illegal in virtually all park areas in Zimbabwe and Namibia   
- [ ] ~~Valuables in checked luggage~~ — jewellery, cameras, electronics, passports, meds: carry-on only   
- [ ] ~~Expensive watches or jewellery~~ — leave them at home entirely   
- [ ] ~~Hard-sided or wheeled luggage for the Mack Air legs~~ — soft bag only, strict dimensions
