7 Places to Find Story Ideas   
Thus, there’s no clear anatomy of a good story idea. As a general rule, however, your idea for a story should answer the following questions:   
- Who is your  [link](https://writers.com/protagonist-definition)   
- What does your main character want?   
- What prevents the main character from getting what they want? (What is the  [conflict](https://writers.com/what-is-conflict-in-a-story)?)   
   
**START WITH THE SIX ELEMENTS OF FICTION**   
- [Plot](https://writers.com/what-is-the-plot-of-a-story): the “what happens” of your story   
- [Characters](https://writers.com/character-development-definition): whose lives are we watching?   
- [Setting](https://writers.com/what-is-the-setting-of-a-story): the world that the story is set in   
- [Point of View](https://writers.com/point-of-view): from whose eyes do we see the story unfold?   
- [Theme](https://writers.com/common-themes-in-literature): the “deeper meaning” of the story, or what the story represents   
- [Style](https://writers.com/writing-styles): how you use words to tell the story   
   
You can read in-depth about each of these in our  [article on the six elements of fiction.](https://writers.com/how-to-start-writing-fiction)
