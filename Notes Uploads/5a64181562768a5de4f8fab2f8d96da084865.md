Lentil Soup   
Please note these are not rigid rules, they are more like guidelines. I generally use things that are in my refrigerator already, so you could theoretically add whatever you like to the pot.   
Let me know if you discover something new and tasty to add to the mix!   
Ingredients:   
1 onion   
1 fennel (can be replaced with a half bunch(?) of celery... or you can have both)   
2 carrots   
4 cloves garlic (can never have too much garlic)   
1bay leaf   
tomatoes (optional - you could also use stewed tomatoes from a can but I like the fresh ones)   
mushrooms (optional)   
kale (optional)   
1 cup dry green/brown lentils (washed)   
4-6 cups veggie broth (depends how liquidy you want it). You could also just use water, then add more cumin or salt   
olive oil (or 2 tablespoons butter if you want to go all out)   
1 tablespoon cumin (not sure if I've ever actually measured it out, so feel free to play around with this)   
lemon   
cilantro   
plain greek yogurt (optional)   
1. Heat oil (or butter) in pot and saute diced onion and fennel and cook until somewhat softened   
2. Add sliced carrots and celery and allow to cook until softened   
3. Add lentils, garlic, veggie broth, diced tomatoes and cumin   
4. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally   
5. Lower heat to simmer and add mushrooms   
6. Simmer until lentils are tender, stir occasionally (sometimes can take a while, maybe 30-60 min?)   
7. Add kale at the end and allow to cook until kale is tender (a few minutes?)   
8. Once served, add lemon, cilantro, and a dollop of yogurt, stir and eat!
