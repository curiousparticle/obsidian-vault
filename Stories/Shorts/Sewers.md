To write a 10-page short story (which is roughly 2,500 words), a **two-week sprint** is the perfect framework. It is long enough to give you breathing room, but short enough that you can see the finish line from day one.

To prevent the story from going "all over the place," we are going to use a highly structured, day-by-day flight plan. Your only job is to do the specific task assigned to that day. No more, no less.

Here is your daily scaffolding contract.

### **Week 1: The Flight Path & The Setup**

_The goal this week is to lay the tracks and write the first half._

*   **Day 1: The Foundation (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**
    *   _Task:_ Define your main character, their setting, and their **one specific goal** for this story (e.g., _An elderly wizard needs to find his misplaced wand before the Ministry inspector arrives at 4:00 PM_). Introduce the character and the problem right away on page one.

**Day 2: Complications Arise (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**

_Task:_ Make the goal harder. Introduce an obstacle. (e.g., _The wizard looks under the sofa, but instead of his wand, he finds a tribble-like magical creature that has started multiplying_).

  

  

*   **Day 3: Rising Stakes (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**
    *   _Task:_ The character tries to fix the problem, but their choice makes things worse. The clock is ticking.

  

  

**Day 4: The Midpoint Shift (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**

_Task:_ A new piece of information is revealed that changes how the character must look at the problem. We are now at Page 4.

  

  

**Day 5: The Strategy Session (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**

_Task:_ The character forms a final, desperate plan to solve the crisis before time runs out.

  

*   **Days 6 & 7: Spacedock (Rest Days)**
    *   _Task:_ **Do not write.** Do not look at the manuscript. Let your creative battery recharge.

### **Week 2: The Climax & The Landing**

_The goal this week is to race to the finish line and execute a controlled landing._

*   **Day 8: The Dark Night of the Soul (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**
    *   _Task:_ The plan from Day 5 fails. All hope seems lost. (e.g., _The inspector's car pulls into the driveway, and the house is now completely full of magical creatures_).

  

  

**Day 9: The Epiphany & Climax (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**

_Task:_ The character realizes the only way out is to use a skill or trait they’ve ignored until now. They execute their final move.

  

**Day 10: The Resolution (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**

_Task:_ The dust settles. Show the immediate aftermath of the climax. Did they succeed or fail? How are they changed?

  

  

*   **Day 11: The Final Image (Goal: 1 Page / ~250 words)**
    *   _Task:_ Write the final scene that mirrors page one but shows the new reality. Write the words **"THE END."** (We are now at Page 10).

  

*   **Day 12: The Celebration**
    *   _Task:_ Treat yourself to a cup of Earl Grey (hot) or a chocolate frog. You have finished a story!
*   **Days 13 & 14: The Revision Protocol**
    *   _Task:_ Only _now_ are you allowed to go back to page one. Read through and smooth out the pacing, fix the typos, and ensure the logic holds up.

### **The Three Golden Scaffolding Rules for this Sprint:**

1.  **The 250-Word Vault:** One page is roughly 250 words. This is a very small number! You can write 250 words in 15 or 20 minutes. Once you hit your one page for the day, **stop writing.** Step away. Save your energy for tomorrow.
2.  **The One-Way Mirror:** You are strictly forbidden from reading what you wrote the previous day beyond the last two sentences. If you read Day 2 on Day 3, you will start editing, lose momentum, and the ship will crash.
3.  **The Bracket Trapdoor:** If you realize on Day 6 that you forgot to give a character a coat, do not go back to Day 1. Write \[NOTE: Give him a coat on page 1\] right there in the text, and keep moving forward.

If you are ready to accept this mission, let's start with **Day 1**. Who is our main character, and what is the immediate, specific problem they need to solve in these 10 pages?
